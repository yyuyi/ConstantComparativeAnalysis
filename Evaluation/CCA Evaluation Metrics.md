# CCA Evaluation Metrics (1-5 Likert Scale)

Use these criteria for the reflective evaluation agent and for human review.

## A. Process Fidelity

- Incident comparison fidelity: Each coded segment includes 2-6 word incident labels, explicit similarities/differences, and an analytic memo.
  Anchors: 1 = missing comparison logic; 3 = comparison present but uneven; 5 = consistent, explicit, method-faithful comparison.
- Full-dataset comparison coverage: The analysis compares recent, retrieved earlier, and anchor/boundary incidents rather than relying only on local sequence.
  Anchors: 1 = mostly local/recent only; 3 = some cross-dataset comparison; 5 = systematic early-late, cross-transcript, and boundary-case comparison.
- Memoing quality: Memos identify tensions, unresolved questions, and theoretical sampling leads.
  Anchors: 1 = descriptive or absent; 3 = useful but shallow; 5 = analytic, comparative, and action-guiding.

## B. Analytic Consistency

- Label-category alignment: Incident labels are coherently synthesized into categories without losing important distinctions.
  Anchors: 1 = categories unrelated to labels; 3 = partial alignment; 5 = categories clearly grow from incident patterns.
- Category distinctiveness: Categories have clear defining properties and non-redundant boundaries.
  Anchors: 1 = highly overlapping; 3 = some redundancy; 5 = crisp boundaries and well-articulated contrasts.
- Synthesis coherence: The final paragraph follows from incidents, categories, and memos.
  Anchors: 1 = unsupported leap; 3 = plausible but under-integrated; 5 = coherent, grounded, and comparative.

## C. Traceability

- Segment traceability: Categories list supporting transcript and segment identifiers.
  Anchors: 1 = weak or missing references; 3 = partial references; 5 = consistent traceable support.
- Quote verification: Supporting quotes are present in source context or transparently replaced by source-segment fallback.
  Anchors: 1 = unverifiable quotes; 3 = mixed verification; 5 = verified or clearly audited quotes.
- Claim support: Major synthesis claims can be traced to incident/category/memo evidence.
  Anchors: 1 = unsupported claims; 3 = partial support; 5 = strong audit trail.

## D. Cross-Coder Integration

- Convergence handling: Similar labels/categories across coders are merged at a useful analytic level.
  Anchors: 1 = one pattern per label; 3 = some merging; 5 = coherent integrated pattern set.
- Divergence handling: Differences across coders are retained as analytic tensions rather than erased.
  Anchors: 1 = ignores divergence; 3 = notes divergence vaguely; 5 = uses divergence to refine categories.
- Integrated summary quality: The unified summary explains relationships and contrasts without introducing unsupported constructs.
  Anchors: 1 = disconnected or speculative; 3 = mostly coherent; 5 = concise, integrated, traceable.
